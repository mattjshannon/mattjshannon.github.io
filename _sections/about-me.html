I am an astrophysicist working at NASA Ames in Mountain View, California. My expertise is in data analysis, Python programming, scientific research, writing, and collaboration. In my work, I utilize machine learning and statistical methods to study the nature of organic molecules in space; my interests are particularly pointed towards the analysis of large-scale astronomical datasets and the ability to find compact representations, and the implications therein.

Beyond my scientific research focus, I am a co-developer of the NASA Ames PAH IR Spectroscopic Database (PAHdb). I also enjoy working on the development of new research-enabling tools, such as pyPAHdb, a streamlined Python version of the Ames database, which I presented at the SciPy 2018 conference in Austin, TX (proceeding).
